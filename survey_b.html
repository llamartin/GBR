Background Information

The Great Barrier Reef stretches over 2,300 km along the Queensland coast. It contains around 3,000 individual reef systems and supports over 1,500 species of fish, 400 species of coral, and significant populations of marine mammals and sea turtles.

Mass coral bleaching occurs when sustained high water temperatures cause corals to expel the symbiotic algae that provide them with nutrients and colour. If temperatures remain elevated for too long, the coral dies. Major bleaching events affected the Reef in 2016, 2017, 2020, 2022, and 2024.

The Australian Government is considering a targeted program to reduce bleaching damage in the most vulnerable sections of the Reef. The proposed program would:

  • Deploy localised cooling and shading interventions on 200 km of high-priority reef (roughly 9% of the Reef's total length)
  • Reduce coral mortality by an estimated 30–40% in the treated areas during future bleaching events
  • Operate for 10 years, after which the program would be reviewed
  • Have no effect on coral bleaching outside the treated areas

Before You Answer

Research shows that people often say they would pay more for environmental programs in surveys than they would actually pay if the program were real. This is called 'hypothetical bias.' Please answer the following question as if the payment were real — that is, as if this program would actually be funded by the tax described below, and you would actually have to pay.

Payment Vehicle and Budget Reminder

If the program goes ahead, it would be funded through an annual levy added to the federal income tax of every Australian taxpayer. This levy would appear as a separate line item on your tax return each year for 10 years.

Please keep in mind that paying this levy would mean you have less money to spend on other things — including other goods, services, and causes you care about.
